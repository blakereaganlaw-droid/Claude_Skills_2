# Standard-work / SOP template (reference)

A fillable template for one repeatable task. Keep it to a page or two; push detail into linked visuals.
Standard work has three classic elements — takt, sequence, and standard in-process stock — plus the key
points and reasons that make it teachable.

## Header
- **Task / standard name:** <…>
- **Trigger (starts when):** <event> · **Ends when:** <done condition>
- **Owner / author:** <…> · **Version / date:** <v_ / yyyy-mm-dd> · **Next review:** <date>
- **Takt or volume context:** <available time ÷ demand = takt, if demand-paced; else typical volume>
- **Standard inputs / WIP to start:** <files, approvals, data, access that must be on hand>

## Step table
| # | Step (what) | Cycle time | Key point (the detail that makes it right) | Reason (why it matters) |
|---|-------------|-----------|--------------------------------------------|-------------------------|
| 1 | <action>    | <min>     | <quality/accuracy/ease point>              | <what goes wrong without it> |
| 2 | <action>    | <min>     | <…>                                        | <…> |
| 3 | <action>    | <min>     | <…>                                        | <…> |

- **What** = the action, in sequence.
- **Key point** = the specific thing that makes the step come out right (a check, a setting, an order).
- **Reason** = why the key point exists — this is what makes the standard teachable and prevents silent
  step-dropping.

## Visual management
- One-page visual work instruction with screenshots/photos of the key points.
- Visual controls: checklist, color-coding, done/not-done board, exception flag, tolerance markers.
- The goal: the correct state is obvious, and a deviation is visible at a glance.
