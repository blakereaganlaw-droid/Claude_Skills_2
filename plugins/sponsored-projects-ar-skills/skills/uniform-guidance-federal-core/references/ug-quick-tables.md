# Uniform Guidance quick tables (reference)

Educational summary of public 2 CFR Part 200 text. **Not a substitute for the regulation, the
Notice of Award, or institutional policy; agency terms (NIH GPS, NSF PAPPG) may be stricter.**
Thresholds below reflect the revision effective for awards issued on/after October 1, 2024 —
older awards may carry prior terms; the award document controls.

## MTDC composition
**Included:** direct salaries and wages, fringe benefits, materials and supplies, services,
travel, and up to the first $50,000 of each subaward (regardless of the period).
**Excluded:** equipment, capital expenditures, patient care charges, rental costs, tuition
remission, scholarships and fellowships, participant support costs, and the portion of each
subaward above $50,000.

## Always-unallowable examples (Subpart E)
| Cost | Citation |
|---|---|
| Bad debts (incl. losses on uncollectible federal AR) | §200.426 |
| Alcoholic beverages | §200.423 |
| Entertainment | §200.438 |
| Lobbying | §200.450 |
| Fines, penalties, damages | §200.441 |
| Fundraising (with narrow exceptions) | §200.442 |
Collected-but-unallowable costs must be refunded, often with interest — §200.410. Overdraws
and other amounts owed become debts to the Federal Government — §200.346.

## Key post-award sections for AR analysis
- **§200.302 Financial management:** the documentation standard billing data must meet.
- **§200.305 Payment:** advances limited to immediate cash needs; reimbursement preferred
  otherwise; timing rules (see the federal-billing-cash-management skill).
- **§200.307 Program income** and **§200.306 cost sharing:** both change what is billable.
- **Closeout (§200.344–.346):** final financial reports, residual balances, and the deadline
  pressure behind award close-out exposure metrics.
