# Effort reporting essentials (reference)

Educational summary of public §200.430 text. The regulation, agency terms (e.g. NIH salary
cap policy), and institutional policy govern.

## Contents
- §200.430 element checklist
- Red-flag scan
- Questioned-cost-to-AR math
- Terms

## §200.430 element checklist
- [ ] **Reasonable:** compensation consistent with what the institution pays for similar work
- [ ] **Uniform policy:** same charging rules for federal and non-federal activities
- [ ] **Records:** supported by internal controls; part of official records
- [ ] **Total activity:** distributions reflect 100% of institutional activity, never more
- [ ] **Distribution support:** records support how salary splits across awards/activities
- [ ] **IBS basis (IHEs):** charges based on institutional base salary, not inflated rates
- [ ] **True-up:** interim budget-based charges adjusted when actuals differ significantly
- [ ] **Caps/cost-share:** agency salary caps respected; over-cap salary borne institutionally

## Red-flag scan (run against project/payroll data)
| Red flag | What it suggests | Where to look |
|---|---|---|
| Person totals >100% across awards | Overcommitment | Effort/payroll distribution by person |
| 100% on one award + known other duties | Total-activity denominator ignored | Role data vs charges |
| Distribution identical every period | Estimates never trued up | Distribution history |
| Certification by remote/unrelated signer | No suitable means of verification | Certification records |
| Salary above agency cap fully charged | Cap not applied | Salary vs cap by agency |
| Mass retroactive transfers near close-out | Chasing money, not effort | Cost-transfer log |
Any hit on a salary-heavy federal award escalates the AR-durability concern below.

## Questioned-cost-to-AR math
When effort support fails on amounts already billed/drawn:
```
Questioned direct salary               S
+ Fringe on S (at applied rate)        F
+ F&A on (S + F) (at applied rate)     I   ← indirects unwind too
= Potential refund / federal debt      S + F + I  (+ interest in many cases)
```
So a $50k questioned salary at ~30% fringe and ~50% F&A is ≈ $97.5k of AR-quality exposure —
the multiplier is why personnel density weights the risk scan. Refund mechanics: §200.410;
debts: §200.346 (see `federal-billing-cash-management`).

## Terms
- **Effort:** the share of total institutional activity devoted to an award (not hours/40).
- **IBS (institutional base salary):** the annual compensation basis for charging.
- **Committed effort:** what the proposal/award promised; monitored against charged effort.
- **Certification:** the after-the-fact confirmation that charges reasonably reflect work.
- **Salary cap:** agency limit (e.g. NIH) on the rate chargeable; excess is cost-shared.
