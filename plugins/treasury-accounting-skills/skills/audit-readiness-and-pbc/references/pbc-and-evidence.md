## PBC tracker format
| # | Audit area | Item description | Owner | Due | Status | Delivered (link/date) | Auditor follow-up |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
Statuses: Not started / In progress / Ready for review / Delivered / Follow-up received / Closed.
Weekly standup during fieldwork: everything not green, by owner. Challenge-at-intake: items
that don't apply, duplicate prior year, or belong to another team get raised in week one, not
silently ignored.

## Treasury/accounting PBC starter list
**Cash:** bank reconciliations (all accounts, period end); complete bank account inventory
(bank, account, signers, purpose); statement copies for tested months.
**Debt:** facility agreements + amendments; debt rollforward (draws, paydowns, balances);
accrued interest calc; covenant compliance certificates + workpapers; amortization schedules.
**Investments:** custody statements; holdings listing with ratings/maturities; investment
policy + compliance check runs; realized/unrealized gain-loss support.
**Hedging:** ISDA list; outstanding trade listing with MTM support; hedge designation
documentation; confirmations for sampled trades.
**Accruals/prepaids:** accrual workpapers with bases; true-up analysis; prepaid amortization
schedules tying to GL; search-for-unrecorded-liabilities (your own cutoff testing).
**Intercompany:** IC matrix at period end; difference schedules and resolutions; IC agreements;
elimination entries with support.
**Close/GL:** trial balance; journal listing (they'll sample); close checklist with sign-offs;
significant estimates memo(s).

## Workpaper standard (definition of done)
- [ ] Header: entity, period, what this schedule is, preparer + date, reviewer + date
- [ ] Every number sourced: system, report name, parameters, run date
- [ ] Ties: totals agree to trial balance / GL, with the tie shown explicitly
- [ ] Judgments and estimates explained where made (basis, alternatives considered)
- [ ] Self-contained: no numbers that require the preparer to explain verbally
- [ ] Sanitization respected if leaving the finance environment

## Walkthrough briefing card (give to process owners)
- Describe what you actually do, step by step, on a real transaction — not the policy summary.
- Name systems and screens; show, don't characterize.
- Identify where you check something before acting (those are the controls; be specific).
- If asked something outside your part: "that's handled by X, I'd be guessing" beats guessing.
- If practice differs from the documented process, say so plainly — the gap will be found
  anyway, and candor now is cheaper than contradiction later.

## Finding response format
| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| Finding (as stated) | Auditor's wording |
| Facts check | Confirmed accurate / factual corrections with evidence |
| Root cause | Process, not person |
| Risk assessment | What could actually go wrong, how material |
| Remediation | Concrete action, owner, target date |
| Interim mitigation | What limits exposure until fixed |
Self-identified issues: disclose proactively with this same format — it converts a potential
finding into evidence that monitoring works.
