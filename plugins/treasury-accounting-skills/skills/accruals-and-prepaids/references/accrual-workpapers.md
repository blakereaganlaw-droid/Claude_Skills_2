## Accrual driver checklist (starter)
- [ ] Open POs with goods/services received, not invoiced (receipt accrual report)
- [ ] Recurring vendor calendar: rent, utilities, insurance, SaaS, telecom — invoice arrived?
- [ ] Payroll: days worked after last payroll date × daily rate (+ taxes/benefits)
- [ ] Bonus/commission plans: period's earned portion per plan mechanics
- [ ] PTO/vacation balance movement (if accrued)
- [ ] Interest on debt: per facility terms through period end
- [ ] Professional fees: open engagements (legal, audit, consultants) — status from owners
- [ ] Freight/duty on in-transit inventory (terms-dependent)
- [ ] Rebates/volume discounts earned by customers, owed by vendors
- [ ] Post-period invoice review: anything dated after close with in-period service dates
Review the list itself quarterly: every true-up "miss" is a candidate new driver.

## Accrual workpaper columns
| Item | Driver/source | Estimation basis | Amount | Auto-reverse? | Account | Preparer | Reviewer |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
Basis examples: "PO 4711 balance for received qty", "avg of last 3 invoices (Apr–Jun)",
"engagement letter fee × 60% complete per counsel email 6/28".

## True-up report
| Vendor/item | Accrued (prior period) | Actual invoice | Error $ | Error % | Note |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
Review monthly: large single errors → fix that estimate's basis; persistent one-direction bias
→ a driver is missing or a basis is systematically stale; accrual with no invoice after 2–3
cycles → stale, investigate and release with documentation.

## Cutoff test format
**Search for unrecorded liabilities (the after-window):** sample invoices posted days 1–20 of
the new period; for each, service/delivery date. In-period service + no accrual = exception.
**Prepaid direction:** sample in-period expense postings above the capitalization floor with
service periods extending past period end; unamortized future portion = exception.
Record: population, sample method/size, exceptions, disposition, conclusion.

## Prepaid amortization schedule
One row per item; workbook total ties to GL monthly.
| Item | Vendor | Total paid | Service period | Monthly amort | Amortized to date | Remaining | Fully amortized (mo/yr) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
Conventions: start amortization in the first month of service (or pro-rate); capitalization
floor documented; multi-year items reviewed annually for impairment/cancellation (a cancelled
SaaS contract's remaining prepaid writes off now, not over the dead contract's life).
