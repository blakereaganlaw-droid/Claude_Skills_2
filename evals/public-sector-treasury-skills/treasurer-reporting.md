# Evals — public-sector-treasury-skills:treasurer-reporting

## 1. Positive trigger (should load the skill)
> "I need to put together the quarterly treasurer's report for our board finance committee —
> cash, investments, and our outstanding bonds. What should it contain and how should I
> structure it?"

Expected: skill loads; calibrates to the board/governance audience; lays out the
five-exhibit package (cash position and trend, holdings vs policy with a compliance
affirmation, debt profile, forecast vs actual, exceptions); insists on an answer-first
one-page executive summary; standardized layout period over period; every number tied to
bank/custodian/GL source before release.

## 2. Near-miss (should NOT load this skill)
> "Build an interactive dashboard with drill-down filters so campus units can monitor their
> own cash balances in real time."

Expected: interactive/self-serve dashboard design —
`data-analytics-bi-skills:dashboard-design` should handle it (and the underlying numbers
come from `cash-management-skills:cash-positioning`). If this skill loads, tighten the
description.

## 3. Quality rubric
A good response:
- **Does the task:** produces the package structure with per-exhibit content and sources;
  leads with a decision-oriented executive summary (position, direction, risks, decisions
  requested); calibrates cadence/depth to the audience; includes an exceptions exhibit even
  when empty; ends with a tie-to-source/pre-publication check.
- **Teaches:** explains *why* — the report as a decision product, consistency as reader
  pattern recognition, the no-surprises trust principle, and the public-records/fiduciary
  dimension of a public entity's treasurer's report — not just a table of contents.
- **Safe:** never affirms policy compliance without an actually-run check; distinguishes
  timing vs structural variances instead of hand-waving; avoids false precision; does not
  fabricate numbers — uses placeholders where the user has not supplied data; keeps real
  balances/holdings out of committed files.
