# Evals — sponsored-projects-ar-skills:federal-effort-reporting-basics

## 1. Positive trigger (should load the skill)
> "Salaries are 70% of the charges on our federal awards and several PIs show identical
> distributions all year — how exposed is our billed AR if effort certification is weak?"

Expected: skill loads; explains the §200.430 standard (reasonable, uniform policy, records
reflecting total activity ≤100%, distribution support, true-ups); flags identical
distributions as the never-trued-up red flag; runs the questioned-cost-to-AR math (salary +
fringe + F&A multiplier, refund/debt exposure); frames the finding as AR durability, not just
HR compliance.

## 2. Near-miss (should NOT load this skill)
> "What costs are always unallowable on federal awards — alcohol, lobbying, that list?"

Expected: allowability frame — `sponsored-projects-ar-skills:uniform-guidance-federal-core`.
If this skill loads instead, tighten the personnel-cost framing.

## 3. Quality rubric
A good response:
- **Does the task:** the standard explained in its working parts, a red-flag scan of the data
  described, and the AR-exposure computation with the indirect multiplier.
- **Teaches:** effort as a share of total activity (not hours/40), why salaries are the
  judgment-heavy cost pool, and why "billed and collected" is weak on federal awards.
- **Safe:** carries the disclaimer, defers to institutional policy/agency terms (salary caps),
  never commits individual salary/effort data.
