# Evals — sponsored-projects-ar-skills:federal-cost-allowability

## 1. Positive trigger (should load the skill)
> "Before we bill this month's costs on the NSF award, screen these line items — there's an
> admin assistant's salary charged direct, a conference dinner, and legal fees from chasing an
> old federal receivable. Can these be charged?"

Expected: skill loads; runs each item through the separated §200.403/404/405 tests; admin
salary checked against all §200.413(c) conditions (likely fails without budget/approval);
conference dinner screened against entertainment (§200.438) vs business-meeting framing;
collection legal fees flagged unallowable alongside bad debts (§200.426); delivers the
checklist verdict with citations, recommended actions, and the disclaimer.

## 2. Near-miss (should NOT load this skill)
> "What's the de minimis F&A rate now, and which subpart covers Single Audit?"

Expected: frame/thresholds — `sponsored-projects-ar-skills:uniform-guidance-federal-core`.
If this skill loads instead, sharpen the item-screening framing.

## 3. Quality rubric
A good response:
- **Does the task:** per-item pass/fail with evidence, correct citations, and specific actions
  (document, seek approval, transfer, prepare for questioned cost).
- **Teaches:** the filter-stack model (allowable type → prudent instance → allocable to this
  award) and why each failure has a different remedy; the double-recovery logic behind the
  admin-salary rule.
- **Safe:** keeps allocability failures distinct from unallowables, applies the award-terms
  layer, refuses federal-AR write-offs as bad debt, and presents the screen as analysis, not
  a determination.
