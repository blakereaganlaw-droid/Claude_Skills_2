# Evals — continuous-improvement-skills:kaizen-and-codesign

## 1. Positive trigger (should load the skill)
> "I want to run a two-day kaizen event to improve our accounts-payable intake process, and I want the
> AP clerks and the people who receive their output in the room designing it. How do I plan and
> facilitate it?"

Expected: skill loads; charters a narrow, measurable, time-boxed event with a sponsor; puts the actual
operators and a downstream customer in the room; starts at the gemba; facilitates for equal voice; runs
rapid PDCA (try on real work same day); captures standard work and a 30/60-day follow-up; applies
respect-for-people throughout.

## 2. Near-miss (should NOT load this skill)
> "Before the workshop, can you help me map the whole procure-to-pay value stream end to end so we know
> where the biggest waste is?"

Expected: this is value-stream mapping that *scopes* a future event, handled by
`continuous-improvement-skills:value-stream-mapping`. Facilitating the event itself is this skill. If
kaizen-and-codesign loads instead, tighten the cross-links.

## 3. Quality rubric
A good response:
- **Does the task:** charters a tight, measurable, time-boxed event; convenes the doers plus the
  downstream customer plus a decision-maker; goes to the gemba; facilitates for equal voice; runs rapid
  PDCA; and closes with standard work and a scheduled follow-up.
- **Teaches:** explains *why* those closest to the work design the most durable improvements and why
  co-design builds the ownership that makes change stick (people support what they help create).
- **Grounded in respect-for-people:** frames problems as process problems not blame, credits
  contributors, and treats the event as seeding a continuous-improvement habit rather than a one-off.
